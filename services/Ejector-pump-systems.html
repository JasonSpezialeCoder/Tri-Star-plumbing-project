EJector pump systems

We repair and install Ejector pump systems, also known as sewage ejector systems or sewage pump systems, which are used to remove wastewater or sewage from lower levels or areas where gravity drainage is not possible. These systems are commonly found in basements, crawlspaces, or areas below the main sewer line.

Ejector pump systems consist of a sewage ejector pump, a basin or pit to collect the wastewater, and a discharge pipe. When wastewater enters the basin, the float switch or pressure sensor activates the pump, which then pumps the sewage or wastewater up and out of the basin through the discharge pipe. The discharge pipe typically connects to the main sewer line or a septic tank.

These systems are necessary in situations where the elevation or location of a building's plumbing fixtures, such as toilets, sinks, or showers, is below the main sewer line. Without an ejector pump system, wastewater would not be able to flow out of these fixtures and would result in backups or flooding.

Ejector pump systems are often found in residential or commercial buildings that have basement bathrooms, laundry rooms, or wet bars. They are also commonly used in areas with high water tables or where the sewer line is located at a higher elevation than the building.

Regular maintenance is required for ejector pump systems to ensure their proper functioning. This includes checking the pump for clogs or debris, inspecting the float switch or pressure sensor, and cleaning the basin or pit to prevent buildup or odors.

Overall, ejector pump systems play a crucial role in safely and efficiently removing wastewater or sewage from areas where gravity drainage is not possible.
